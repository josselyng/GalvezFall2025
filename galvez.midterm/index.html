Sunscreen

About

There is a lot of mixed opinions of sunscreen and it isn't until recently, society has seen an importance with using it. You either know someone who is crazy about sunscreen and smear it on every day and night, or the person who has never purchased one in their lives.

Also known as sunblock, sun lotion or sun cream, sunscreen is a photoprotective topical product that helps protect our skin from sunburn and prevent skin cancer. Sunscreens come as lotions, sprays, gel, powder, sticks, and foam. Active ingredients like zinc oxide, homoslate, titanium dioxide, and more are protecting your skin from the sun's UV rays. Depending on the ingredients, there are two types of sunscreen, mineral or chemical sunscreen.

Benefits

Sunscreen's only purpose is to protect the skin from UV rays therefore, it can help prevent skin cancers like, melanoma and squamous cell carcinoma. A study from 2013 showed that the application of suncreen everyday, can slow or temporarily prevent wrinkles and sagging skin. This study involved 900 white people in Australia and had them apply sunscreen everyday for four and a half years. The people that followed through had more smoother and elastic skin than the ones who didn't follow through. 32 of these subjects also showed the daily use of sunscreen reversed premature aging. Sunscreen can improve the skin's appearance and texture.

UVA AND UVB

To better understand what sunscreens really do, let's talk about the actual UV rays and how much damage they can really do. UVA and UVB are two types of ultraviolet (UV) radiation from the sun. UVA rays have a longer wavelength which means it penetrates deeper into the skin and is associated with premature aging. UVB is still damaging as it has a shorter wavelength, causing sunburn which can cause skin cancer.

SPF/Broad Spectrum

Sunscreen can seem simple but, some people are confused by what SPF means on the packaging as well as broad spectrum. Some sunscreens have both while others have either or. SPF and broad spectrum both measure the sunscreen's effectiveness. SPF measures how much protection is provided by the sunscreen. SPF is associated with UVB therefore, it indicates the level of sunburn protection. Broad spectrum helps protect against both forms of UV radiation so it's ideal to find a sunscreen that provides SPF and broad spectrum. While it is more common to find suncreen with both elements, broad spectrum's ingredients are expensive and harder to formulate, therefore, there are few products out there without it.
