Sunscreen

Which is right for you?

There are two different types of sunscreen.
Mineral and chemical sunscreen.
These differ due to the type of ingredients and way they work.

Mineral Sunscreen

Known as organic or physical sunscreen, mineral sunscreen has a heavier and thicker texture than chemical sunscreens. It has active ingredients like zinc oxide and titanium dioxide.
These ingredients work as a shield against sun rays, sitting on your skin's surface and deflecting the UV rays from your skin.
When applied, mineral sunscreens leave a white tint on the skin but, works immediately upon application.

Chemical Sunscreen

Chemical sunscreens contain chemical ingredients like avobenzone, oxybenzone, and octinoxate. These chemical ingredients have chemical compounds that work as a sponge to absorb the UV rays and convert them into heat, which is released from the skin.
Due to the chemicals, this type of suncreen may cause allegric reactions or irritation.
Chemical sunscreen is transparent when applied and takes some time to be absorbed into the skin before it starts working/becomes effective.
